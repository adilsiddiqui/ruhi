Cooking Tips

We want you to relish our snacks and cherish every bite of our sweets that gives you a lingering taste and a lasting memory of that moment. Hence we have tried to bring to you a simple but savored suggestions and recipe to complement our dishes.

A nice and refreshing cup of Indian Tea

In a pot add 100ml water along with 20gms of sugar, 3gms
of tea powder. Place the pot on the burner & bring it to a
good boil.

Once the water starts to boil, add in 150ml of full fat milk
(preferably buffalo milk). Bring it to a rolling boil.

Once the milk come to a boil, add a pinch of cardamom
powder to the tea.

Simmer for 30 seconds and strain and enjoy your cup of a
traditional Indian tea!

To enjoy masala milk cold or hot

Begin by lightly crushing 5 almonds, 3 cashews, 6 pistachio; add a
pinch of saffron, a pinch of cardamompowder and a dash of grated
nutmeg. Keep aside.

In a pot add a glass of milk along with 1 1/2 teaspoon sugar and
place it on a burner and heat. Once the milk is hot enough, take it off
the burner and add the nut mix.

Give it a little swirl and allow it to cool. Once the milk has cooled,
refrigerate it overnight.

Blend the milk before serving. Garnish with some almond flake,
pistachio flakes and a few strands of saffron.
Enjoy it chilled on a summer afternoon or heat it warm for a cozy winter night!
